# Four-question alignment filter

> Source seed: inline (Romanian) — Pilon 1 framing per Narcis's call. Cele 4 întrebări = filtru de craft pentru ce skill/agent merită shipped vs killed.
> Recommended variant: **Spicy (B)** — pilon-1 register cere contrarian builder voice; hook în primele 7 cuvinte e cel mai tare; structura cu cele 4 întrebări lovește direct fără filler.

## Variant A — Plain (6 tweets)

1/ Alignment vs motivation. Same look at week 1. Different outcomes at week 12. The skills I ship vs the skills I abandon — four questions tell me which is which: (158 chars)

2/ One. Do you have to convince yourself to start it? If yes, you're running on willpower. Willpower runs out at week 3. (117 chars)

3/ Two. Do you lose track of time inside it? If no, every hour costs you. The cost compounds. (90 chars)

4/ Three. Does it energize you or drain you? Drain is fine for a sprint. For a year of building, drain kills you. (110 chars)

5/ Four. Would you do it for free? The skills I'd build for free — those compound. The ones that need salary, hype, or paid features to keep moving — they break. (159 chars)

6/ Four out of four — alignment. One or two — motivation. Motivation breaks. Every time. (86 chars)

## Variant B — Spicy (6 tweets)

1/ Most builders don't know if they're aligned or just motivated. They find out at week 3, when motivation runs out and the project dies. Four-question filter: (158 chars)

2/ One. Do you have to convince yourself to start? If yes, you're forcing it. Forced work doesn't ship 100 skills. Ships 3, quits. (127 chars)

3/ Two. Do you lose track of time inside it? If you keep checking the clock, that's the project telling you it's not yours. (121 chars)

4/ Three. Does it energize or drain you? Drain is the price of motivation. Energy is the signal of alignment. Same on day 1, opposite by day 30. (142 chars)

5/ Four. Would you do it for free? If the answer needs paid features, hype, or "but I could monetize it later" — kill it. That's not your project. (144 chars)

6/ Four for four = alignment. One or two = motivation. Motivation always breaks first. (84 chars)

## Variant C — Reflective (6 tweets)

1/ I'm 51. 30 years of code. Killed more projects than I shipped. Now I have a filter. Four questions that decide whether I keep going or move on. (144 chars)

2/ One. Do I have to convince myself to start? If yes, I'm not aligned. I'm performing. (85 chars)

3/ Two. Do I lose track of time inside it? Some projects pull me in for hours. Others, the clock crawls every minute. That's the diagnostic. (138 chars)

4/ Three. Does it energize or drain me? At 51, drain isn't a phase. It's expensive. I don't have 22-year-old recovery time. (121 chars)

5/ Four. Would I do it for free? Most projects fail this. The ones that pass are the ones that compound. The rest run on hype and burn out. (137 chars)

6/ Four out of four, you found it. One or two, you're running on motivation. Motivation breaks. Always. (102 chars)
