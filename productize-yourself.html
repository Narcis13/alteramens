The Core Formula

Specific Knowledge + Leverage + Accountability + Judgment

↓ 10 ideas that encode this framework

01
Idea #01

Play Compounding Games

  • All returns — wealth, relationships, knowledge — come from compound interest
  • Pick a game worth playing long-term. Every direction change interrupts the compounding.
  • Choose an industry you love, a craft to master, and people to grow with

"Pick an industry where you can play long-term games with long-term people."

— Naval
02
Idea #02

Specific Knowledge

  • Society wants it but can't train for it yet — if they could train you, they could replace you
  • Found by pursuing genuine talent, passion, and curiosity — not whatever is hot right now
  • Feels effortless to you; looks effortful to others

"Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like work to others."

— Naval

04
Idea #04

Develop Judgment

  • Wisdom applied to external problems — knowing long-term consequences of your actions
  • Direction matters more than speed. Judgment gets the direction right.
  • Leverage magnifies the consequences of every decision you make

"In an age of infinite leverage, judgment becomes the most important skill."

— Naval
05
Idea #05

Take Accountability

  • Take risks under your own name — this gives you skin in the game
  • Fail publicly → take the critique. Win publicly → take the credit.
  • Act with high integrity. People forgive failures; they don't forgive dishonesty.

"Embrace accountability, and take business risks under your own name. Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage."

— Naval
06
Idea #06

Work That Feels Like Play

  • The secret to consistency is alignment — when you love the work, you never stop
  • You don't need a vacation from it. You could do it forever.
  • Best creators chase alignment, not motivation

"The goal is not to retire. It's to find work that feels like play to you but looks like work to others."

— Naval
07
Idea #07

Authentic Creation

  • Competition is an illusion. The only fair comparison is with your past self.
  • Nobody can compete with you at being you — especially in an AI world
  • Authenticity eliminates jealousy: when you do what you're meant to do, you want nothing else

"Escape competition through authenticity."

— Naval
08
Idea #08

The Right Skillset

Reading Foundation of all learning
Writing Foundation of all content
Arithmetic Foundation of reasoning
Persuasion The multiplier — talking and selling
Code ★ "The closest thing we have to magic" — Naval
09
Idea #09

Pick the Right Partners

  • Intelligence — prevents incompetence
  • Energy — prevents laziness
  • Integrity — prevents betrayal

If any one is missing → it won't work.

"Pick business partners with high intelligence, energy, and, above all, integrity."

— Naval
10
Idea #10

Stay Off the Hedonic Treadmill

  • Most people upgrade lifestyle as income grows — they never feel free
  • Money's real goal: not buying stuff, but buying time. Don't let people buy yours.
  • Freedom first: time, work, location, schedule — then the nice stuff

"People who live far below their means enjoy a freedom that people busy upgrading their lifestyles can't fathom."

— Naval
