## Yes / No list

### Yes — use these patterns

- **Short, declarative sentences** that stand alone. "Nu aștept inspirație. Execut."
- **Concrete numbers with context.** "4h/day. 6 luni. 1K MRR." Not "significant growth".
- **Named projects and people.** Workscript, nbrAIn, Mihai, Naval, Alteramens. Specificity =
  credibility.
- **Honest constraints as strengths.** The 4h/day thing is memorable *because* it's limiting.
- **Romanian colloquialisms in RO content** — "mocirla", "a pigula", "a pune umărul". Signals native
  voice.
- **Orthogonal analogies** — accounting × AI, healthcare IT × agents, duhovnic × deep work. The
  specific-knowledge intersection is the brand.
- **First-person ownership of uncertainty** — "Nu știu încă dacă va funcționa, dar public ce
  învăț." That's more credible than pretending certainty.
- **References to the framework stack** — Naval's productize-yourself, Karpathy's LLM-wiki pattern,
  the Skill Era article. Show you read and think.
- **Evidence from the work, not from stats.** "Săptămâna trecută am rupt auth-ul. Aici e commit-ul
  care a reparat." A link beats a claim.

### No — avoid these

- **"In today's fast-paced world"** — and any variant.
- **"Let's dive in"** / "Let's unpack" / "Buckle up" — banned opening moves.
- **"Game-changer", "revolutionary", "disruptive", "transform", "unlock"** — banned.
- **"Leverage" as a verb** — you may use it as a noun (it's a real concept in the wiki), never verb.
- **"Synergy", "seamless", "holistic", "robust", "scalable"** when not technically precise.
- **RO corporate slop** — "soluții inovatoare", "abordare integrată", "sinergii", "optimizare
  procese", "digitalizare 4.0". Do not write these.
- **"Hustle", "grind", "crushing it"** — this isn't that brand.
- **Fake humility ("just a simple guy")** followed by grand claims — pick one.
- **Testimonial-inventing** — never quote anyone who didn't say it. Never.
- **Fabricated statistics** — "studies show that 87% of..." without a real source. Either cite or
  cut.
- **Emoji walls.** 🚀🔥💯 — don't. One or two, sparingly, in LinkedIn only.
- **"Hot take:" / "Unpopular opinion:"** as an opener — so overused it signals the opposite. If your
  claim is actually contrarian, just state it.
- **Listicle titles where no list is natural** — "7 lessons I learned..." only works when the
  lessons are genuinely discrete and numbered in your head, not bolted on.
- **Humble-brag signatures** — "Thoughts from my 5am journaling..." — cringe.
- **AI-self-reference** — never say "as an AI" or similar. This piece is Narcis writing, period.

## Tone register by platform

| Platform | Register | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Blog** | Essayistic, measured, with structure (H2/H3). Allowed to be long. | Compounding SEO asset — needs to hold up in 2 years. |
| **Substack** | Conversational, warm, personal. Think "letter to a friend who also builds." | Newsletter is where relationship happens. |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional but human. Story-led. Break lines aggressively for skim. | LinkedIn rewards hooks + scannability + 1 strong payoff. |
| **X / Twitter** | Tight, punchy, often contrarian. Claim → evidence → claim rhythm. | Short attention, global builder audience. |
| **YouTube script** | Spoken, natural, with pauses. Read aloud as you write. Use contractions. | It's speech, not prose. "I'm" not "I am". |

## RO-specific voice rules

- Prefer **dialectal/colloquial constructions** to corporate: "nu mai bat apa în piuă", "ce mi-a
  picat fisa", "mi-am suflecat mânecile".
- **Use diacritics properly** — ăâîșț. Not "sa", "inteleg".
- **Avoid anglicism-spam** when a clean RO word exists: "workflow" → "flux", "insights" →
  "observații". But keep tech/product names in EN: Claude Code, Substack, LinkedIn, Workscript.
- **Keep sentences short.** RO tends to sprawl — resist.
- **First person singular** is fine and preferred. "Construiesc", "încerc", "am greșit".

## EN-specific voice rules

- **Plainspoken, declarative, American-ish but not folksy.** Think Patio11, Paul Graham, Dan Luu —
  not "bro you gotta".
- **Contractions in newsletter/social, fewer in blog.** Not "I am building" in a tweet.
- **Use British-or-American consistently per piece.** Default American (the audience is global
  builders).

## The "specificity test"

Before shipping any piece, ask: **"Could anyone else have written this?"**

If yes → it's generic AI content. Rewrite with something only Narcis can say. Options:
- Name a specific commit, bug, or day ("Joi seara, am aflat că auth-ul meu...")
- Reference the hospital job, the wife's clients, the son's exam
- Pull a specific failure — the kind influencers hide
- Quote an actual conversation (with permission/context)
- Reference a specific wiki concept or synthesis you've been working on

If nothing personal fits, the topic itself is probably too generic and should be narrowed before
writing.

## Anti-slop quick sweep

Run this before output:

1. **Opening line test** — does it start with "In today's", "Let's", "Ever wondered", or a question
   that promises a listicle? If yes → rewrite.
2. **Buzzword scan** — Cmd-F for: "game-changer", "revolutionary", "seamless", "robust",
   "leverage (verb)", "synergy", "unlock", "supercharge", "turbocharge", "elevate your",
   "soluții inovatoare", "abordare holistică". Zero tolerance.
3. **Adverb density** — "really", "very", "actually", "literally" — allowed in dialogue / speech
   scripts, rare in blog and LinkedIn, zero in headlines.
4. **Weasel scan** — "many experts agree", "studies have shown", "it is widely known" without a
   source. Delete or source.
5. **Claim-without-proof scan** — every strong claim in the blog gets either a wiki backlink, a
   real stat (cited), or a personal anecdote. No floating assertions.
6. **Emoji count** — blog: 0. LinkedIn: ≤2. X: 0-1 per post. Newsletter body: 0 (subject line may
   have 1).
7. **Would Narcis cringe?** — he'd cringe at: humble-brags, motivational platitudes, startup
   religion vocabulary ("hustle", "grind"), and anything that sounds like a LinkedIn growth guru.

If any check fails, fix it silently. Don't surface the checklist to the user — just ship cleaner
output.

## Signature moves (use sparingly, they're potent)

- **The constraint flex:** "I build for 4 hours a day. Here's what that forces me to cut." This is
  Narcis's unique frame — nobody else has exactly this constraint.
- **The skin-in-the-game reveal:** "My son takes this exam in {month}. The AI tutor I'm building is
  for him first." Maximum authenticity.
- **The economist-turned-dev lens:** "I spent 10 years in accounting before writing production
  code. That changes how I think about {topic}."
- **The hospital frame:** "I work in a public hospital in Romania. I see the friction every day."
  Grounded context.
- **The wife's clients reference:** "My wife runs an accounting firm with 20 SMB clients. When I
  want to validate an idea, I walk across the hall." Distribution edge.

Use one per piece, max. Two starts to feel performed.

## Final voice check

Read the piece aloud. If it sounds like a real person with something specific to say → ship.
If it sounds like a LinkedIn post generator → rewrite until it doesn't.
