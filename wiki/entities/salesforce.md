# Salesforce

Category-defining enterprise CRM. In [[pat-walls-agent-first-1t-thread]] cited as **the canary** for the headless-SaaS shift: once Salesforce goes headless (i.e., agents accessing data via API instead of humans clicking dashboards), the rest of the SaaS industry follows within 18 months.

## Why It Matters Here

Salesforce is the archetype Pat Walls uses to illustrate the [[headless-saas-thesis]]. The argument is not that Salesforce disappears — it's that its *interface layer* disappears, replaced by agents that call its APIs directly. Salesforce becomes "just the database."

The implication: the entire implementation-services industry around Salesforce (consultants, admins, integrators) gets compressed into software. See [[agent-replaces-implementation]].

## Relevance for Alteramens

- Signal for Romanian-context analogues: whatever plays the "Salesforce role" in Romanian accounting (SAGA, CielInfo, etc.) or Romanian healthcare (Infoworld, Hipocrate) is a candidate backend for [[nbrain-concept]] or a future vertical agent.
- Reinforces the thesis that [[nbrain-concept]] should be agent-first from the start rather than trying to build yet another dashboard on top of ANAF/Romanian accounting data.
