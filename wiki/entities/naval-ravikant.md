# Naval Ravikant

Angel investor, philosopher, and entrepreneur. Co-founder of AngelList. Known for his Twitter thread "How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky)" which became a foundational framework for internet-era wealth creation.

## Why He Matters to Faber

Naval's "Productize Yourself" framework is the **philosophical foundation** of the entire Alteramens project. Every decision filter maps to his four pillars: [[specific-knowledge]], [[leverage]], [[judgment]], [[accountability]].

## Key Ideas

- **[[productize-yourself]]** — The synthesis: specific knowledge + leverage + uniqueness + accountability
- **[[specific-knowledge]]** — Can't be trained for; found by pursuing genuine curiosity
- **[[leverage]]** — Code and content are permissionless; you don't need anyone's permission
- **[[judgment]]** — Direction > speed; wisdom applied to external problems
- **[[accountability]]** — Take risks under your own name; skin in the game
- **[[compounding-games]]** — Pick a game and don't interrupt the process
- **[[work-as-play]]** — Work that feels like play = the ultimate unfair advantage
- **[[authentic-creation]]** — Nobody can compete with you at being you

## Quotes

> "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."

> "Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your genuine curiosity and passion rather than whatever is hot right now."

> "In an age of infinite leverage, judgment becomes the most important skill."
