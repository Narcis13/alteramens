# Productize Yourself — Naval's 10 Ideas on Getting Rich

Summary of Naval Ravikant's framework as curated by Stijn Noorman. Source: Twitter/X thread, saved in vault as [[../Foundation|Foundation]].

## Core Thesis

Naval's framework for getting rich (without luck) distills to one phrase: **"Productize Yourself."** It combines four elements: specific knowledge, leverage, accountability, and judgment. The key insight is that these compound — you need all four working together.

## The 10 Ideas

### 1. Play Compounding Games
Pick an industry, craft, and people for the long term. Every direction change interrupts compounding. Growth is slow at first, then accelerates. The key is not to interrupt the process.

### 2. Specific Knowledge
Knowledge you can't be trained for. If society can train you, it can replace you. Found by pursuing talents, passions, and curiosity — not what's hot. Building it feels like play to you, looks like work to others.

### 3. Build Leverage
Four types: people, money, content, code. Code and content are **permissionless** — you don't need anyone else. Leverage is a force multiplier: bigger outcomes from the same input.

### 4. Judgment
Wisdom applied to external problems. Direction matters more than speed. Ways to improve: collect mental models, focus on first principles, control emotions, prioritize understanding over memorization.

### 5. Accountability
Taking risks under your own name. Skin in the game. How you build reputation. Double-edged: failures are public, but wins build credibility. High integrity + genuine effort = people forgive failures.

### 6. Work as Play
When work feels like play, consistency becomes effortless. Chase alignment, not motivation. The barrier between work and play fades. This is the ultimate unfair advantage.

### 7. Authentic Creation
Escape competition through authenticity. Nobody can compete with you at being you. Compete with your past self, not others. Authenticity eliminates jealousy and distraction.

### 8. Right Skillset
Five skills that compound across every field: writing, reading, arithmetic, persuasion, programming. These never go out of style and scale with technology.

### 9. Right Partners
High intelligence + high energy + high integrity. All three required. Low energy = laziness, low integrity = betrayal, low intelligence = incompetence.

### 10. Hedonic Treadmill
Live below your means. Freedom > stuff. The goal of money isn't to buy things — it's to not have people buy your time.

## Synthesis Formula

> Productize = Specific Knowledge + Leverage
> Yourself = Uniqueness + Accountability
> Combined with Judgment = Unstoppable
