# Bounded Problem Wedge — Narrow Beats Generic

A product that targets a sharply bounded problem (specific buyer, specific deadline, specific content scope, specific willingness to pay) wins against a product targeting a vague category, even when the bounded version looks smaller on paper.

## The Principle

Vagueness is the enemy. "AI personalized learning platform" is a category where 100+ startups die per year. "AI tutor for Carol Davila admission, Romanian biology curriculum, July 2027 cohort" is a product with:

- Identifiable buyers (Romanian parents of high-school seniors targeting medicine)
- Pre-validated willingness to pay (existing meditation market at 5-10k EUR per cohort)
- A fixed deadline that creates urgency (admission date)
- A finite content scope (specific manual + barem)
- A clear success metric (admitted vs not admitted)
- A natural marketing message ("get into Carol Davila")

Each of those properties enables a downstream decision (pricing, marketing, content scope, success criteria) that is impossible when the problem is vague.

## Tests for "Bounded Enough"

A problem is sufficiently bounded if you can answer all of these in one sentence each:

1. **Who buys?** (named segment, not "businesses" or "students")
2. **Why now?** (event, deadline, mandate, or pain trigger)
3. **What replaces?** (what alternative did they buy last year?)
4. **How much do they pay today?** (existing budget for the alternative)
5. **What does success look like?** (binary or thresholded outcome)
6. **What is the content scope?** (finite list, not "everything in domain")

If any answer requires hand-waving, the wedge is not bounded yet. Narrow further.

## Why This Beats "Big TAM"

The "big TAM" instinct is to maximize addressable market. The bounded wedge instinct is the opposite: minimize addressable market until the path to revenue is unambiguous, then expand from a beachhead. The beachhead generates real cash, real testimonials, real moat. The big-TAM version generates pitch decks.

## Applied to AI Tutor for Medical Admission

Initial framing: "AI personalized learning platform." Reframed to: "AI tutor for Carol Davila admission, biology, 2027 cohort." Each narrowing made the next decision easier:

- Pricing: matches meditation market (600-900 EUR / package)
- Content: one manual, one barem, finite question bank
- Marketing: parents in Romanian search "meditatii admitere medicina"
- Validation target: a known student (Mihai) and a known date
- Compounding path: chemistry → other UMF → other Eastern European med schools

See [[brainstorm-ai-tutor-medicina]] for the full reframe.

## Anti-Pattern: "Platform"

Whenever a founder says "platform," ask which bounded problem comes first. The platform is what you might become; the wedge is what you actually build. Building the platform first is the most reliable way to build nothing.
