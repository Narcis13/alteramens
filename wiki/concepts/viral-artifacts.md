# Viral Artifacts — Products That Market Themselves

Design your product's output to be inherently shareable. Users distribute your brand for you because sharing the artifact serves THEIR goals (status, identity, utility).

## The Pattern

The canonical example: Spotify Wrapped. Users share their music stats not because Spotify asked them to, but because the artifact says something about their identity.

Formula: **Product usage → personalized artifact → user shares → new users see → loop.**

## Characteristics of Viral Artifacts

1. **Personal** — reflects something unique about the user
2. **Visual** — easy to share on social media
3. **Status-signaling** — makes the sharer look good/interesting
4. **Low-friction** — one click to share, no extra effort
5. **Branded** — your product name is embedded naturally

## Application Ideas

- Dev tools: "Your code stats this month" (lines written, languages used, streak)
- SaaS: Usage dashboards designed for sharing, not just viewing
- AI tools: "Questions I asked AI this week" summaries
- Healthcare IT: anonymized benchmarks ("your hospital vs average")

## Why It's Leverage

Viral artifacts are [[leverage]] — you build the artifact generator once, and every user becomes a distribution channel. Zero marginal cost per share.

## Risks

- Forced virality feels inauthentic — the artifact must serve the user first
- One-time spikes vs sustained distribution — Wrapped is annual, not daily
- Privacy concerns when the artifact reveals too much about usage
