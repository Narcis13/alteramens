# Judgment — Direction Over Speed

Wisdom applied to external problems. Knowing the long-term consequences of your actions and acting accordingly.

## Why Judgment Matters Most

> "Judgment is making decisions. Leverage magnifies the consequences of those decisions. In an age of infinite leverage, judgment becomes the most important skill." — [[naval-ravikant|Naval]]

Most people optimize for speed. But direction matters more. A fast runner going the wrong way loses to a slow walker going the right way.

## How to Improve Judgment

1. Keep life simple
2. Collect mental models
3. Control interpretations of events
4. Focus on long-term consequences
5. Prioritize understanding over memorization
6. Make hard decisions instead of easy ones
7. Focus on first principles — always question everything
8. Control emotions — a calm mind makes better decisions

## Judgment in the Skill Era

The [[skill-era]] thesis: LLMs compress execution. What remains scarce is judgment. This is what [[encoded-judgment]] means — encoding a way of thinking into a skill, not just a function.

**API = mechanical function.** "Here's how to send an email."
**Skill = encoded judgment.** "Here's how to audit a landing page like a serious growth operator."

## Alteramens Application

Judgment manifests as:
- **[[validate-before-build]]** — Don't build before the market says so
- **[[kill-fast]]** — Kill ideas that don't show life signs
- **Revenue > vanity** — Only what generates revenue matters
- Choosing to focus on Romanian accounting niche (where unfair advantages exist) over global market
