# Distribution Over Product — Access Beats Quality

When building is cheap (AI, vibe coding, no-code), the scarce resource shifts from engineering to distribution. A mediocre product with great distribution beats a great product with no distribution.

## The Shift

| Era | Bottleneck | Winning Move |
|-----|-----------|--------------|
| Pre-AI | Building the product | Technical skill |
| Post-AI (vibe coding) | Getting users | Distribution strategy |

200K+ projects launch daily on platforms like Lovable. Most die in obscurity. The differentiator is not code quality but market access.

## Implications for Builders

1. **Allocate time to distribution before launch** — not after
2. **Choose distribution-native architectures** — [[mcp-as-distribution]], [[programmatic-seo]]
3. **Validate demand first** — see [[validate-before-build]]
4. **Leverage beats effort** — automated distribution (SEO, AEO, viral artifacts) over manual outreach

## Connection to Skill Era

In the [[skill-era]], distribution means being embedded in agent workflows. If your skill/tool is called by AI assistants, you have distribution without marketing. This is the purest form of distribution-over-product: the product IS the distribution channel.

## Tension

This doesn't mean product doesn't matter — it means product alone isn't enough. The anti-pattern is "build and they will come." The principle is: build AND distribute simultaneously.

## Agent-Native Era Sharpening (2026-04)

[[pat-walls-agent-first-1t-thread]] takes this thesis further: when agents + APIs make building trivial, distribution collapses into audience + brand. The founder's media surface becomes the permanent moat because product moats get commoditized.

See [[media-plus-agents-distribution]] for the founder-level expression. The through-line: each wave reduces the cost of building; each wave raises the premium on owned distribution. The last wave rewarded pretty UIs on ugly data; this wave rewards smart agents on exposed APIs — and both waves reward whoever already owned the audience.
