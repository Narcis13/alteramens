# DRI with Agents — Temporary Teams Around Goals

DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) adapted for AI-native orgs. The "individual" can be an agent team, not a person. Borrowed from [[jack-dorsey]]'s framework, implemented at [[single-grain]].

## How It Works

1. Pick a specific business goal
2. Spin up a temporary team — humans + agents assigned to that goal
3. Give it a deadline (90 days)
4. The team either hits the target or dissolves
5. Agents return to the general pool
6. Learnings get absorbed into the [[world-model]]

The organization gets smarter from every project, including failures.

## Why It Works

- Clear accountability (one DRI per goal)
- Time-bounded (no permanent bureaucracy)
- Agents are elastic (assign to DRI, then reclaim)
- Knowledge doesn't leave when the team dissolves — it stays in the world model

## Practical Application

[[eric-siu]]'s advice: pick your top three business goals, create a DRI for each, assign agents, set a deadline, measure. Any company can steal this immediately.

## Relevance to Alteramens

For solo/small operations, DRI translates to: one focused initiative at a time, Claude Code as the agent team, 2-3 week sprints (aligned with [[kill-fast]]), learnings captured in Faber regardless of outcome.
