# Kill Fast — Archive Without Attachment

If an idea shows no signs of life after 2-3 weeks of genuine effort, archive it and move to the next. No attachment to sunk cost.

## The Rule

> "If after 2-3 weeks of effort an idea doesn't show signs of life → archive and next."

Signs of life: someone expresses interest, someone asks to pay, someone uses it, engagement increases, a clear path to revenue emerges.

## Why It Matters

Time is the scarcest resource (~10 hours/week). Spending it on dead ideas has massive opportunity cost. The compounding clock ([[compounding-games]]) ticks on ideas with traction, not on ideas you wish had traction.

## The Trap

Emotional attachment to an idea. "But I've already built X..." is sunk cost fallacy. The market doesn't care how much time you've invested.

## Complementary Principle

Kill fast on individual ideas, but DON'T kill the game itself. The meta-game (building profitable software with AI leverage) stays constant. Individual ideas within that game get tested and killed quickly.

## No Hobby Projects

> "No hobby projects that don't compound toward 1K MRR."

This is harsh but necessary given the time constraints and the 6-month deadline.
