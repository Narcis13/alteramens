# Specific Knowledge

Knowledge that society wants but doesn't know how to get. You can't be trained for it — if you could, society would train someone else and replace you.

## How to Find It

- Pursue your genuine curiosity and passion (not what's hot)
- Observe what you spend time on naturally
- It feels effortless to you but looks effortful to others
- "Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like work to others"

## How to Develop It

1. Analyze your existing skills
2. Combine skills in unique ways
3. Follow curiosity relentlessly
4. Observe your natural actions
5. Double down on strengths

## Narcis's Specific Knowledge

The rare combination of:
- **Economist** (ASE) + **10 years accounting experience** → deep understanding of the domain
- **Self-taught developer** → can build the solutions
- **IT administrator in healthcare** → understands institutional pain
- **Wife is accounting expert** → immediate market access and validation

This combination is hard to replicate. Nobody else has this exact intersection.

## Connection to [[skill-era]]

In the Skill Era, specific knowledge is what you encode into skills. The scarcity isn't execution (LLMs handle that) — it's the judgment that comes from deep domain expertise.
